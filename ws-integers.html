    Exercise Group.

    Answer the following by comparing the two values.
    5.
    Which is warmer: \(-3\)°F or \(-5\)°F?
    6.
    How much warmer is \(20\)°F than \(-10\)°F?

    7.

    Rewrite each statement to use negative numbers instead of positive numbers, changing terms if needed to convey the same information.
    (a)
    We were \(100\) feet below sea level.
    (b)
    The brewing company had a \(\$1{,}500\) loss in 2015.
    (c)
    The temperature outside was \(12\)°F below zero.

    8. The Number Line.

    (f)

    9. Multiplying Integers.

    Write each multiplication as an equivalent addition problem. Make up a context for it. Two examples are provided.
    Example A: \(4\cdot3 = 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12\text{.}\)
    I made \(3\) quilts per month for \(4\) months, making \(12\) quilts in total.
    Example B: \(3(-15) = (-15) + (-15) + (-15) = -45\text{.}\)
    I bought \(3\) movie tickets for \($15\) dollars each, spending a total of \($45\) dollars.
    (a)
    \(5(-7)\)
    (b)
    \(-3(14)\)
    (c)
    \(-5 \cdot 12\)
    (d)
    \((-20)(40)\)

    10. Exploration.

    (a)
    What can you say about a negative number multiplied by a positive number?
    (b)
    What can you say about a negative number multiplied by a negative number?

    Comparing Integers.

    Think about where each pair of numbers is on the number line and circle the correct statement comparing them. Then fill in the mathematical symbol (\(\lt\) or \(\gt\)) showing the relationship.
    11. 2 and 5.
    (a)
    \(2\) is to the (left/right) of \(5\) on the number line.
    (b)
    \(2\) is (less than/greater than) \(5\text{.}\)
    (c)
    \(2 \qquad 5\text{.}\)
    12. 3 and -3.
    (a)
